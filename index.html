Our Process

New Custom Homes

The Design Process - New Custom Homes

1. Planning & Pre-Design

Initial Project Meeting -

To meet you, get an understanding of your thoughts and see if we'd be a good fit. We would discuss your ideas, exchange questions and samples, and get an overall idea of each other.

Coordination & Consultation -

We go over your thoughts, ideas and wishes in more detail and formulate the logictics and how to implement them into the design.

Zoning Analysis & Project Specifictions -

To formualte the peramaters we must work within and to go over each aspect of the house in more detail. We'd create a document laying out each item clearly so it's documented and ready to be put into the design.

Confirmation of Initial Specifications -

This gives you time to make changes to the specifics of the project before the majority design work is completed.

2. Schematic Design

Site Plan & Data -

To show how the house will sit on the lot with a detailed data table to show that we are meeting all the zoning regulations required.

Rough Coloured Exterior Concepts -

To show the basic shape and colour scheme without any details added. This gives a general representation of the house for your consideration before proceeding.

Detailed Floor Plans -

Showing the layout of the house in detail, complete with dimensions, room sizes, fixtures to-scale furnature and with space planning and flow taken care of.

3. Design Development

Continued Refinements & Changes -

Likely, the design won't be perfect at the beginning. This is where you get to 'red pen' the drawings and tailor them to exactly what you'd like.

Detailed Elevations & Perspectives -

The exteriors change from a rough concept to a highly detailed model with siding and trims figured out, railings, features, and everything that makes the house pop.

Interior Modelling -

The interior is the space you'll live in. Modelling it and showing it in 3D is how you will know the space is right for you. You'll digitally stand in the house and look around in every direction.

Exterior & Interior Renders -

Rendered still images of important areas of the house to show you exactly how the space will look and feel before we proceed to finalizing the drawings.

4. Construction Documents

Construction Drawings for Pricing, Other Professionals & Builders -

The drawings you have approved are now turned into finalized construction drawings, designed to convey to the builder and any other professional all the necessary information required to accurately construct your new home. Our construction drawings have been fine-tuned and refined to be builder-friendly and easy to read from years of listening to builder's requests and comments. We take pride in following up on each new home project, both during construction and after completion.

Building Permit Application Drawings -

Drawings meet all the check list items for the building permit application process for the municipality/region the project is located in. The drawings become a legal document with the city and are kept on record forever.

Additions

The Design Process - Additions

1. Planning & Pre-Design

Initial Project Meeting -

To meet you, get an understanding of your thoughts and see if we'd be a good fit. We would discuss your ideas, exchange questions and samples, and get an overall idea of each other and the project.

Coordination & Consultation -

We go over your thoughts, ideas and wishes in more detail and formulate the logictics and how to implement them into the design.

Zoning Analysis & Existing Structure -

To formualte the peramaters we must work within and to create an accurate representation of the existing structure

Confirmation of Existing Drawings -

Another site meeting to confirm measurements, obtain details of structure and take reference pictures. We also go over the project once more and confirm everything prior to design for accuracy.

2. Preliminary Drawings

Site Plan & Data -

To show where the house is and how all new portions will sit in relation to it. A detailed data table to show that we're meeting all the zoning regulations required.

3D Views -

Additions are best visualized with at least one 3D view. All drawings of this nature come with black and white 3D views to accompany the elevation drawings.

Elevation Drawings -

Elevations of all four sides dressed up with trims, siding and any features to show how the project will look in the end.

Detailed Floor Plans -

Showing the layout of the project in detail highlighting the difference between old strucutre and new. The floor plans include dimensions, room sizes, fixtures to-scale furnature and with space planning and flow taken care of.

3. Design Development

Continued Refinements & Changes -

Likely, the design won't be perfect at the beginning. This is where you get to 'red pen' the drawings and tailor them to exactly what you'd like.

2nd & 3rd Preliminary Drawings

The exteriors change from a rough concept to a highly detailed model with siding and trims figured out, railings, features, and everything that makes the house pop.

Final Changes Before Construction Drawings

The interior is the space you'll live in. Modelling it and showing it in 3D is how you will know the space is right for you. You'll digitally stand in the house and look around in every direction.
